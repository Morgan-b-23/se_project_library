Reader picks

That our readers love

  • Enders game

    This is one of the books that I constantly come back to. Its plot is about a young boy named Ender Wiggins who's sent to a military force academy in space to train as an elite officer. We follow along the story of how he overcomes obstacles and eventually find his true place in the academy. It's a classic for a reason and a short read. So if you like sci-fi and a bit of a coming-of-age story with a nice twist at the end I would recommend.

    Orson Scott Card

  • The richest man in Babylon

    This is more of a self-help book that talks about a fiance through a collection of parables. I picked it up while wanting to learn how to make make money work for you, not the other way around. It's another short read and gives good advice on how to be responsible with your finances through rich and interesting stories.

    George S. Clason

  • Frankenstein

    This is another classic, for good reason, That follows the story of Dr. Frankenstien and the hollowing downward spiral of his life after his curiosity and obsession get the best of him. This book is more of a horror fiction novel that I would also suggest to read it if you haven't already. It's a very captivating and emotionally moving story that will have you coming back for a second read.

    Mary Shelley

  • Burial Rites

    Burial Rites is a historical fiction novel based on a true story. It follows the story of Agnes who has been sentenced to death for murder. We go through her past and the events leading up to the "murder" that ultimately would make her a part of the last public execution of a woman in Iceland. This book has more of a dark mystery feel to it, and if you are better with sad endings than I am this would be a great book to pick up.

    Hannah Kent

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.
